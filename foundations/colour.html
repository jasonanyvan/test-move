Colour

4 key colors, the core of each audience or product, are the basis for our palettes and are used in our UI as key visual indicators that give each part of our ecosystem a unique flavour.

We've taken the approach of dynamic color schemes so that our products, apps and platforms can retain and include colors that help define and differentiate from the rest, while also giving users clear cues that tie back to AnyVan's core brand

Tokens ensure the same style values are used across design files and code.

A scheme is defined as a group of tones / colours assigned to specific role, product or audience that get mapped to components and patterns

Primary

The primary identifing colour for an audience or product
Secondary

A supporting colour that helps create visual interest and break up sections or content
Tertiary

An additional supporting colour

Active

A bold colour that acts as, and is often used to indicate active states, hovers or highlights
Surface

Used primarily as the fill colour of cards and other surfaces that house content
Background

The background of the application or execution

The following guidelines outline when to use colours in products, interfaces and messaging. Semantic colour helps users quickly identify a status, an actions, locate help and support and understand next steps

Consistency in colour usage also keeps cognitive load low and makes for a more unified and engaging experience
